Who is Steve Jobs?

Steve Jobs was an American business magnate, industrial designer, investor, and media owner. He was the chairman, CEO, and co-founder of Apple Inc., chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar, a member of the board of directors of The Walt Disney Company after the acquisition of Pixar, and the founder, chairman, and CEO of NeXT. Jobs is widely known as a pioneer of the personal computer revolution in the 1970s and 1980s, along with Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak.

Steve Jobs Timeline

  • 1976

    One of the co-founders of Apple Computer alongside Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. In the same year, the company sold the Apple I in the form of a kit for $666.66.

  • 5 June 1977

    Apple II was released, the first commercially available personal computer in a plastic enclosure with color graphics - and Apple's first successful personal computer.

  • 24 January 1984

    Two days after the $1.5 million Super Bowl advertisement "1984," directed by Ridley Scott, introduced Macintosh to much fanfare during the Apple shareholders meeting, Steve Jobs declared, "For the first time ever, I'd like to let Macintosh speak for itself." The computer's voice then said, "Never trust a computer you can't lift." Macintosh became the first commercially successful small computer with a graphical user interface.

  • 12 September 1985

    CEO John Sculley engineered Jobs' dismissal from Apple. Jobs resigned as the leader of Apple, stating in a board meeting, "I have been thinking a lot, and this is the time for me to move on with my life. Clearly, I have to do something. I'm 30 years old." Shortly after, Jobs started NeXT Computer (which later became NeXT Software), funded by selling his $70 million worth of Apple stock. An "interpersonal" NeXT workstation, with a built-in Ethernet port, was used by Tim Berners-Lee at CERN to become the first server of the World Wide Web.

  • 3 February 1986

    For $10 million, purchase the Graphics Division of Lucasfilm, which became Pixar Animation Studios.

  • 1976

    Steve Jobs became the president and CEO of Pixar. In that year, Jobs introduced Pixar publicly one week after the release of "Toy Story," with Tom Hanks voicing Woody and Tim Allen as Buzz Lightyear. The film grossed $192 million at the box office. Its success helped make it appealing for celebrities to lend their voices to animated characters.

  • 10 December 1996

    Returning to Apple, as an advisor, after purchasing NeXT for $429 million.

  • 10 November 1997

    Introducing the Apple Store, which allows customers to order Apple products directly from the company online.

  • 23 October 2001

    Introducing iPod:

    "With iPod, Apple has invented a whole new category of digital music player that lets you put your entire music collection in your pocket and listen to it wherever you go...With iPod, listening to music will never be the same again."

    Apple sent over 300 million iPods in October 2011.

  • 2 February 2006

    iTunes sells billions of songs:

    "I hope that every customer, artist and music company executive takes a moment today to reflect on what we've achieved together during the past three years."

    Apple sent over 300 million iPods in October 2011..

  • 9 January 2007

    Introducing iPhone:

    "iPhone is a revolutionary and magical product that is literally five years ahead of any other mobile phone...We are all born with the ultimate pointing device--our fingers--and iPhone uses them to create the most revolutionary user interface since the mouse."

    Apple captured 19.1 percent of the smartphone market as of August 2011, after shipping 108 million iPhones worldwide in March 2011.

  • 15 January 2008

    Introducing the App Store as an update to iTunes. Also introducing the MacBook Air. The App Store facilitated 18 billion app downloads on October 4, 2011.

  • 27 January 2010

    Introducing the iPad. Apple sold 500,000 iPads during their first week in the market and captured 84 percent of the tablet market by the end of the year, with an estimated 12.9 million units shipped by December 10, 2010, and 25 million shipped worldwide by June 2011. In the same month, the company revealed that they had sold 200 million iOS devices.

  • 6 June 2011

    Delivering the keynote address at Apple's Worldwide Developers Conference, this was Jobs' final public appearance at an Apple event.

  • 9 August 2011

    Apple's stock has surpassed Exxon Mobil's to become the most valuable company in the US, in terms of market capitalization, at $337.17 billion.

  • 24 August 2011

    Resigning from the position of CEO of Apple and becoming Chairman.

"For the past 33 years, I have looked in the mirror every morning and asked myself: 'If today were the last day of my life, would I want to do what I am about to do today?' And whenever the answer has been 'No' for too many days in a row, I know I need to change something."
